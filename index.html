Why We Started

Born from a feeling
we all know too well

In 2024, I became a victim of a financial fraud. What came after was worse than the fraud itself — the feeling of not knowing what to do. Who to approach? Which form to fill? What are my rights? Nobody tells you these things.

That feeling — helplessness — it wasn't new. As a kid who earned a scholarship but was denied it without explanation. As an 11-year-old sitting inside a radiotherapy center, where the board said "only adults allowed" and nobody stopped to think. As someone navigating death certificates for a family member — figuring out where to apply, how long it takes, how much it costs. Even in death, the system demands you figure it out alone.

2024 brought it all back. But this time, it stayed. For months.

Most of us have been through something where the system failed us and we just didn't know what to do next. We Googled. We asked friends. We felt lost for not knowing basic things about our own rights.

Vitark was born from that feeling. Not from a business plan — from a deeply personal need to make sure no one has to feel that helpless again when it comes to understanding the law.

— Hemant Chhabra Founder, Vitark.ai
